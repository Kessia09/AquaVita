Turning school greywater into small garden & learning

We are a group of students at Rwanda Coding Academy. We built a small bio-filter system that cleans water from our sinks, then we use that water to grow herbs and vegetables in vertical towers.

how it works our small wins

Why we started AquaVita at school

Greywater from sinks goes to waste Some students lack fresh veggies/herbs We wanted to apply coding + IoT to a real problem

So we designed a small, manageable system — perfect for our school campus.

Our system

Mini bio-filter

We built a small filter using gravel, sand, and charcoal. It cleans greywater from our school kitchen sink.

Vertical garden

We grow basil, mint, kale and a few other veggies in recycled towers. Water from the filter feeds them. We get fresh leaves for school meals.

A small step against flooding? Every liter we reuse means less water going into drains. In Kigali, even small actions help reduce runoff. Our little filter might inspire bigger ideas.

What we’ve seen so far

Many positive and helpful changes for our school community.

Fresh herbs in meals

We add mint, basil and kale to the school kitchen. A few students already feel it’s nice to have something green and fresh — plus it's free.

Learning by building

We learned to solder, code Arduino, and measure water quality.

Sharing with families

When we have extra harvest, we give small packs to nearby families. Nothing huge, but it makes us feel useful.

We're working with our teachers and hope to involve more RCA students next term.
