## 📖 About the Project

**AquaVita** (Latin: *aqua* = water, *vita* = life) is a student-built sustainability system at **Rwanda Coding Academy**. We collect greywater from our school kitchen sinks, filter it through a simple bio-filter (gravel, sand, charcoal), and use the cleaned water to irrigate a small vertical aeroponic tower where we grow herbs like mint, basil, and kale.

We also added **Arduino-based sensors** to monitor water flow and soil moisture — it's a fun way to practice coding while solving a real campus problem.

> 🧑‍🎓 This is a **school project**, not a large commercial solution. We're learning by doing, and sharing our journey so others can try too.

## 🚰 The Problem We Noticed

| Issue | What we saw at RCA |
|-------|---------------------|
| 💧 Water waste | Sinks in the kitchen and wash areas send hundreds of liters of lightly-used water directly to drainage every day. |
| 🥬 Nutrition gaps | Some students don't get enough fresh greens or herbs in their meals. Buying organic is expensive. |
| 📡 No real IoT practice | We wanted to apply our coding skills to something tangible — not just simulations. |
| 🌧️ Runoff | In Kigali, heavy rains cause flooding. Reusing water reduces pressure on drains (small step, but meaningful). |

## 🌱 Our Solution

AquaVita is a **closed-loop micro-system** with three simple parts:

1. **Mini Bio-Filter**  
   - Layers: gravel → coarse sand → activated charcoal → fine sand  
   - Cleans greywater from the kitchen sink enough for plant irrigation (not drinking water).

2. **Vertical Aeroponic Tower**  
   - Made from recycled PVC pipes and buckets  
   - Grows: mint, basil, kale, spinach, and a few medicinal herbs (moringa, aloe)  
   - Water from the filter is pumped to the top and mists the roots.

3. **Arduino Sensor Kit**  
   - Soil moisture sensor + water level sensor  
   - Simple LCD display showing pump status and moisture  
   - Optional: data logging to a laptop (CSV export).
